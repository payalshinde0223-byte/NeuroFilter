About This Tool

What Is Internet Addiction?

Internet addiction disorder (IAD) describes compulsive internet use that impairs daily life functions. Recognized by health professionals worldwide, it affects people of all ages and backgrounds.

~6% of the global population Avg. 6.5 hrs screen/day Linked to anxiety & depression Disrupts sleep in 73% of users

This tool is for educational purposes and should not replace professional medical advice.

Warning Signs to Watch For

Spending increasing amounts of time online, failed attempts to reduce usage, neglecting personal relationships, and using the internet to escape negative emotions.

The Science Behind Addiction

Social media and apps exploit dopamine reward loops — notifications, likes, and infinite scroll are engineered to maximize engagement, creating compulsive checking behaviors.

Recovery Is Possible

With awareness, structured limits, and healthy offline alternatives, most people can significantly improve their relationship with technology within 30–60 days.

How NetSense Helps

Our evidence-informed scoring model evaluates both time-based and behavioral indicators, giving you a clearer, more holistic picture of your digital wellness profile.
